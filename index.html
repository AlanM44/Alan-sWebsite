Education

I am in my first year at Oxford Brookes University, where I've expanded my engineering abilities to include industry specific skills as well as automotive knowledge. Before coming to Brookes, I studied Mechanical Engineering for two years at Oregon State University and gained a strong foundation in engineering principles and practices.

Hobbies

Outside of my professional life, I am deeply involved in two main hobbies:

  • Formula Student: As a member of the Oxford Brookes Racing Formula Student team, I contribute to the design, build, and testing of high-performance electric race cars. This experience has honed my skills in teamwork and engineering, giving me a practical way to apply what I've learned. OBR's reputation and involvment in the Motorsport industry has given me critical insight into Motorsports as well as connections with current industry professionals.
  • Mountain Biking: I enjoy the thrill and challenge of mountain biking. It's a great way to stay fit, explore nature, and push my limits.

Past Projects

Throughout my career, I have worked on several noteworthy projects. Some highlights include:

  • Development of a custom suspension system for a Formula Student car, which improved handling and performance.
  • Fabrication of car components through manual machining.
  • Steering mounting on a Formula Student Car.
  • Various GMAW and SMAW welding projects, including the completion of a SMAW structural welding class at Portland Community College
  • In my Maths and Modeling class at Brookes we completed a group MATLAB Project that described the motion of a kinematic system.
  • Hand calculations, CAD and Finite Element Analysis of suspension clevises for a Formula Student Car.
